About-Me

I'm Rhaenyra Targaryen, the cruel

Rhaenyra Targaryen (97–130 AC), the firstborn heir of King Viserys I Targaryen, was a dragonrider and pivotal figure in the Targaryen civil war, the Dance of the Dragons. Named heir in her youth and bonded to the dragon Syrax, her claim was challenged after her father’s remarriage to Alicent Hightower, whose sons sought the throne. Rhaenyra’s marriages—first to Laenor Velaryon and later to Daemon Targaryen—fueled political strife, while rumors about her sons’ legitimacy deepened opposition. When her half-brother Aegon II usurped the throne, she waged a brutal war, briefly ruling as queen before her tragic downfall. Though her reign was short, her legacy endured through her son Aegon III, who eventually restored the dynasty. Rhaenyra’s story remains a gripping tale of ambition, defiance, and the high cost of power in a realm unwilling to accept a queen.
